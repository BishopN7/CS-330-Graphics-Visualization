# CS-330-Graphics & Visualization
This repository will cover what I have learned as far as computer graphics and visualization. 

**How do I approach designing software?**
My approach to designing software is usually always by going by consumer requirements. It
helps me to stay on top, of what needs to be on the priority list. By having a criteria list,
it helps me plan out how to go about designing parts of a program, instead of having to figure out,
what best suits a user and their needs or even a multitude of people. 

**What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?**
When it came to design skills, one of the skills that I enjoyed was applying textures 
and materials to my objects. I figured that getting to decide how I want my 3D scene to turn out,
is the fun part because that's when I can get creative. Another design skill that I have is also,
visualizing how I want my scene to look. By definition, I can imagine how I want a certain world to look,
based on other online concept art. Pinterest is a great resource, if you want to look at artist concepts. 

**What design process did you follow for your project work?**
Following back from my previous courses, I remembered to always invest in research, analysis, and planning, 
because it gives me the battleground to decide how I want to approach my work. When it came to my 3D scene, I knew that
I needed more than just a wine bottle as the focal point of my project. You also have to think about the culture of a scene. 
In other foreign countries, they have various designs for how the interior of a home looks. Countries like Italy for example, 
are more traditional for how they want they want everything to look. So, for my project, I wanted to go for a traditional style 
of living room attire. The scene that I chose was a startng point on how I wanted to further proceed. 

**How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?**
When it comes to future work, I want to try and actually plan out my ideas in a document, or some sort of planning board.
Some people also sketch their ideas to see how their scene should be. I also mentioned how game development was another idea, I 
had in mind as a future career, or even just for fun. In additon, would be to also ask people on how I could design a character,
or get inspiration. But, the two skills I want to apply on a seriou note, are timing and patience. Improper timing and low-patience are
what ruin a scene to look magnificent to a certain audience, and so I want to ensure that it doesn't happen.
